## Q1. Should students choose their major based on passion or job opportunities?

In my opinion, it really **depends on the person**. If someone comes from a wealthy family, they can follow their passion freely without worrying too much about money. But if you are the **breadwinner** of your family, in all likelihood you should choose a major with good **job opportunities** first, and keep your passion as a side [interest]{ /ˈɪn.trɪst/}. So there isn't one right answer — it depends on each person's situation, talent, and goals.

---

## Q2. Do you think taking a vacation is necessary? Why or why not?

Yes, I think taking a vacation is **necessary**. After working hard for a long time, everyone needs to escape from their daily routine and recharge. A short holiday helps reduce stress and gives you more energy and motivation when you come back to work. That's true, but you don't need to spend a lot — even a quiet weekend **off the beaten path** can really **hit the spot**.

---

## Q3. Public transport vs private vehicles: which should people use more?

I think it **depends on the city**. In big cities like Hanoi or Saigon, people should use **public transport** more to reduce traffic congestion and pollution. However, in smaller towns where the bus system is still weak, **private vehicles** are simply more convenient and flexible. So the best answer is a mix — strong public transport in big cities, and private vehicles where there is no good option yet.
