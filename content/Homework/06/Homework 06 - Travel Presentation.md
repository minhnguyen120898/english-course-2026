# 🎤 Pair Presentation — "Why Do Some People Never Travel?"

**Course:** English Lower Intermediate **Topic:** Travel (Recap) **Tags:** #english #presentation #speaking #travel #lower-intermediate

🔗 Lesson: [[Lesson 06 - Recap (Lessons 1-5)]] 🔗 Homework: [[Homework 06 - P1]]

---

> 🎯 **The task:** Speak for **2–3 minutes**. **No papers in your hands** — you must memorize it. There will be a **Q&A** after. Focus on three reasons: **money, fear & comfort zone, and health/age & duties.**

---

## 🗣️ Full Script (~2.5 minutes)

**[Intro — open with a question]**
Hello everyone. Before we begin, let me ask you a quick question — please **raise your hand**: who here loves travelling, or travels whenever they get the chance? _(Pause — look around at the hands.)_ Wow, quite a lot of us! Travel sounds wonderful to most people — but the truth is, a lot of people never travel at all. Today my partner and I want to explain three main reasons why.

**[Reason 1 — Money]**
The first and biggest reason is **money**. Travelling can really **cost an arm and a leg** — flights, hotels, food, and travel insurance all add up quickly. For many families, that money is needed for more important things, like rent, school, or saving for the future. So they decide that a holiday is simply a luxury they can't afford right now.

**[Reason 2 — Fear & comfort zone]**
The second reason is **fear and comfort**. Some people are afraid of flying, or afraid of getting lost in a place where they don't speak the language. Others just don't want to **step out of their comfort zone** — they feel safe and happy at home, so a long flight, jet lag, and a strange culture sound stressful rather than exciting. For them, staying home is the easy choice.

**[Reason 3 — Health, age & duties]**
The third reason is **health, age, and responsibilities**. Older people or people with health problems often find long trips too tiring or even risky. And many adults are simply too busy — they are the **breadwinner** of the family, or they have young children and elderly parents to take care of. When you have so many duties, there is little time or energy left to travel.

**[Conclusion]**
So in conclusion, people don't avoid travel because they are lazy. Usually it's because of money, fear, or their health and responsibilities. Maybe if travel becomes cheaper and easier in the future, more of these people will finally get the chance to explore the world. Thank you for listening — we're happy to take your questions.

## 🧠 Cue-Card Skeleton (memorize THIS, not the full text)

Remember only the keywords. The full sentences will come naturally once you know the order.

1. **Intro** → ✋ "Raise your hand — who loves travelling / travels often?" (pause, look around) → "Travel sounds wonderful, but many never travel — 3 reasons."
2. **Money** 💰 → cost an arm and a leg → flights/hotels/food → needed for rent, school, savings → a luxury.
3. **Fear & comfort** 😨 → afraid of flying / language → won't step out of comfort zone → home feels safe -> a long flight, jet lag, and a strange culture -> stressful rather than exciting.
4. **Health, age & duties** 🧓 → trips tiring/risky → too busy → breadwinner / children + parents.
5. **Conclusion** ✅ → "Not lazy — money, fear, health/duties. Cheaper travel → more people explore. Thank you."

> Memory trick: **3 reasons = 💰 😨 🧓** (Money → Fear → Health). If you remember those three icons, you remember the whole talk.

## ❓ Q&A Preparation (likely questions + short answers)

**Q: Do you think money is really the main reason?**
> Yes, I believe so. For most people, if travel were free, they would do it. Fear and health matter, but money is the first wall.

**Q: How could people travel if they have very little money?**
> They can travel locally, go off the beaten path near home, take a red-eye or budget flight, and avoid peak season. Travel doesn't have to be expensive.

**Q: Can fear of travel be overcome?**
> Yes. People can start small — a short domestic trip with a friend — and slowly step out of their comfort zone until they feel confident.

**Q: Isn't "no interest in travel" also a reason?**
> That's true. Some people are simply content at home and feel no desire to travel — but often that comes from the same fear or comfort we mentioned.

**Q: What about people who are too busy with work?**
> Exactly — that's part of our third reason. When you're the breadwinner with many duties, time and energy run out before money does.

**Q: Which reason is the hardest to solve?**
> Probably health and age, because money can be saved and fear can be reduced, but you can't easily change someone's health or family responsibilities.

## 🎬 Delivery Tips (no papers!)

- **Rehearse out loud 4–5 times**, not in your head. Record yourself on your phone and listen back.
- **Practise the hand-over** with your partner at least twice so it feels smooth.
- Speak **slowly and clearly** — pause after each reason. Pauses make you sound confident, not nervous.
- **Make eye contact** and use your hands for "first, second, third."
- If you forget a word, **don't freeze** — paraphrase: _"…the money problem, you know?"_ and keep going.
- For Q&A: it's fine to start with _"That's a good question. In my opinion…"_ to buy a second to think.
