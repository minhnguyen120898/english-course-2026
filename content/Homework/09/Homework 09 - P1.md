## Q1. What part of your job causes you the most stress? Why?

For me, the most stressful part is working to a **tight deadline** while juggling many tasks at the same time. I'm a developer, and my system runs 24/7, so if something breaks suddenly, I have to fix it right away — even at night. On busy days I feel completely **swamped**, and I'm always a little afraid to **drop the ball** and miss something important. I used to panic in these moments, but now I try to stay calm and solve one problem at a time.

---

## Q2. What motivates you to complete your tasks at work?

What motivates me most is the feeling of solving a difficult problem and seeing my product actually work. I really enjoy building something useful and learning new technology along the way. I also like to **take initiative** — when I finish my own tasks, I feel proud and ready for the next challenge. And of course, keeping my team **on the same page** and not letting them down is a strong motivation too.

---

## Q3. If you could choose your boss, would you choose your current one? Why or why not?

Yes, I think I would choose my current boss. He gives me a lot of freedom to work in my own way and trusts me to **sort things out** by myself, which I really appreciate. We're usually **on the same page** about what is important, so we rarely have misunderstandings. He's not perfect, of course, but he respects my time and lets me grow — so for me, that's a good boss.
