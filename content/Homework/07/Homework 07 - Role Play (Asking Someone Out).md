# 🎭 Role Play — Asking Someone Out (Online)

**Course:** English Lower Intermediate **Topic:** Social Media **Tags:** #english #roleplay #speaking #social-media #lower-intermediate

🔗 Lesson: [[Lesson 07 - Social Media]] 🔗 Homework: [[Homework 07 - P1]]

---

> 🎯 **Situation:** Two people match online. **Person A (the man)** wants to ask **Person B (the woman)** out for coffee. However, **B is unsure** and prefers to get to know him better before meeting in person. Goal: A stays polite and confident; B says "not yet" without being rude.

---

## 💬 Sample Dialogue (~1.5 minutes)

**A:** Hey, nice to match with you! 😊 I really liked your profile — your travel photos are amazing.

**B:** Aw, thank you! That's sweet. I saw you're into photography too.

**A:** Yeah, it's my hobby. Actually… would you like to grab a coffee sometime this week?

**B:** Oh, that's a kind offer… but to be honest, I'd like to get to know you a little better first. I hope that's okay.

**A:** Of course, no pressure at all. I totally understand — we just started chatting.

**B:** Thank you for understanding. I've had people **ghost** me before, so I prefer to take things slowly.

**A:** That makes sense. How about this — let's chat here for a week, and if you feel comfortable, we can plan that coffee. Okie dokie?

**B:** Okie dokie! That sounds perfect. Let's keep talking and see how it goes.

**A:** Great. So… what's the first app you open every morning? 😄

**B:** Ha! Probably Instagram — I always check the trending topics. What about you?

---

## 🗣️ Useful Phrases

### Person A — Asking someone out (polite & confident)

- _"Would you like to grab a coffee sometime?"_
- _"Are you free this weekend? I'd love to meet up."_
- _"No pressure, but if you're comfortable, maybe we could meet?"_
- _"How about we chat for a bit first, then plan something?"_

### Person B — Saying "not yet" politely

- _"That's a kind offer, but I'd like to get to know you better first."_
- _"I'm a little shy about meeting in person so soon — I hope that's okay."_
- _"Can we take things slowly? I prefer to chat for a while first."_
- _"Maybe later — let's keep talking and see how it goes."_

### Both — Keeping it friendly

- Agreeing casually: _"Okie dokie!"_, _"Sounds good!"_, _"That works for me."_
- Reassuring: _"No pressure at all."_, _"I totally understand."_
- Showing interest: _"Tell me more about…"_, _"What do you like to do for fun?"_

---

## 🧩 Lesson Vocabulary You Can Slip In

- **Ghost someone** — _"I've been ghosted before, so I take it slow."_
- **Trending topic** — _"I open Instagram to check the trending topics."_
- **Emoji** — use a friendly emoji to keep the tone light. 😊
- **FOMO** — _"I almost didn't reply because of FOMO — glad I did!"_
- **Flex** — (joke) _"I won't flex, but I do make great coffee."_

---

## 🔄 Swap Roles & Variations

Practise **both roles** so you're ready for either. Try these variations to keep it fresh:

1. **B says yes** — but suggests a video call first instead of meeting.
2. **A is nervous** — practise asking again after a shy first attempt.
3. **B is busy** — politely reschedule: _"This week is hard, but how about next Saturday?"_

---

## 🎬 Tips

- Keep it **natural and warm** — smile while you speak; it changes your tone.
- **A:** ask, then immediately add _"no pressure"_ — it sounds confident, not pushy.
- **B:** soften a "no" with a reason + a door left open (_"…but let's keep talking"_).
- Use short **follow-up questions** to keep the conversation alive instead of letting it die.
- React with the lesson's casual phrase: **"Okie dokie."**
