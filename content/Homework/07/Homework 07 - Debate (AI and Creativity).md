# ⚖️ Debate — Do AI Tools Reduce Creativity & Critical Thinking?

**Course:** English Lower Intermediate **Topic:** Social Media / AI **Tags:** #english #debate #speaking #ai #lower-intermediate

🔗 Lesson: [[Lesson 07 - Social Media]]

---

> 🎯 **Warm-up debate:** _"AI tools reduce your creativity and critical thinking skills — agree or disagree?"_ Prepare **both sides** so you're ready for whichever you're given. Be ready to make a point, then defend it.

---

## ✅ AGREE — "AI does reduce them"

1. **We stop thinking for ourselves.** If AI gives the answer instantly, we don't struggle with the problem — and struggling is how critical thinking grows.
2. **Less original creation.** When AI writes, draws, or codes for us, we produce less of our own work, so our creative "muscles" get weaker over time.
3. **We trust it too easily.** People often accept **AI-generated content** without checking it, instead of questioning whether it's true or biased.
4. **Same ideas for everyone.** AI tends to give average, similar answers, so our work can become less unique and more copy-paste.

> Strong line: _"If we never struggle to find an answer, we never learn how to think."_

---

## ❌ DISAGREE — "AI does not reduce them (it can help)"

1. **It removes boring work.** AI handles the repetitive parts, freeing our time for higher-level thinking and bigger ideas.
2. **It's a creative partner.** AI can give us starting points and options; choosing, combining, and improving them still needs human creativity.
3. **It depends on how you use it.** A calculator didn't make people worse at maths — used well, AI is a tool, not a replacement for thinking.
4. **More access to ideas.** AI lets us explore topics and viewpoints we couldn't reach before, which can actually grow our thinking.

> Strong line: _"AI doesn't kill creativity — lazy habits do. The tool depends on the user."_

---

## 🔄 Rebuttals (answering the other side)

- **They say "AI makes us lazy"** → _"That's true for some people, but the problem is the habit, not the tool. A pen can't write a good essay by itself either."_
- **They say "AI is just a helper"** → _"I don't really agree — when the helper does 90% of the work, the human stops practising the skill."_
- **They say "we still choose and edit"** → _"Maybe, but over time many people stop editing and just accept the first answer."_
- **They say "it saves time for big ideas"** → _"That's a fair point, however, only if people actually use that time to think — many just move on."_

---

## 🗣️ Useful Debate Phrases

**State your side:** _"In my opinion…"_, _"I strongly believe that…"_, _"I'm on the side that…"_

**Agree / disagree / in between** (from [[Lesson 06 - Recap (Lessons 1-5)]]):

- ✅ _"I agree because…"_
- ❌ _"I don't really agree because…"_
- 🤝 _"That's true, but…"_ / _"That's a fair point, however…"_

**Give reasons & examples:** _"The main reason is…"_, _"For example…"_, _"Let me give you an example…"_

**Conclude:** _"So, to sum up…"_, _"That's why I believe…"_

## 🎤 Sample 30-Second Opening (pick your side)

**Agree:**
> In my opinion, AI tools can reduce our creativity and critical thinking. The main reason is that AI gives us answers instantly, so we stop struggling with problems — and struggling is exactly how we learn to think. For example, if a student lets AI write every essay, in all likelihood they will slowly lose the skill to write and argue by themselves. That's why I believe we must use AI carefully.

**Disagree:**
> I don't really agree that AI reduces creativity and critical thinking. In my opinion, it depends on how we use it. AI can do the boring, repetitive work, which frees our time for bigger ideas. For example, an artist can use AI for quick drafts, then add their own style and judgement. That's why I believe AI is a tool — it grows your thinking if you stay in control.

---

## 🌟 My Core Argument — "It Depends on Your Foundation"

This is the strongest version of your idea — use it as your main point:

> AI doesn't reduce creativity and critical thinking by itself — **it depends on the foundation you already have.**

**Point 1 — It can make it harder for students to get a job.**
> For university students, leaning on AI too much can be dangerous. While they study, AI makes everything easy — but after graduation, they may find it **harder to get a job than before**. AI now does many of the simple, entry-level tasks, so companies need fewer juniors. And if a graduate relied on AI instead of building a strong base of knowledge, they can't show the deeper thinking that employers still want — so they struggle to compete.

**Point 2 — It's powerful for people who already have a strong base.**
> On the other hand, for someone who already has good base knowledge, AI is a powerful tool. They can tell whether the answer is correct, fix its mistakes, and use it to work much faster — so AI grows their output instead of replacing their brain.

**Point 3 — Example (developers).**
> For example, an experienced developer with strong architecture thinking can control AI far more effectively than a beginner. The senior knows what good design looks like, so they guide the AI and catch its errors. A junior who can't yet think for themselves just copies whatever the AI gives — mistakes included.

**Conclusion line:**
> So I'd say AI doesn't reduce these skills — it **amplifies what you already have**. It weakens beginners who skip the basics, but it makes strong thinkers even stronger. That's why building your own foundation first matters more than ever.

---

## 💡 Quick Take (for the Q&A)

_"AI is like a powerful tool — it reduces thinking for those who skip the basics, but boosts it for those with a strong foundation. The real skill now is knowing when to use it and when to think for yourself."_

---

## 🌍 Big-Picture Closing Line

A calm, wise way to end your turn:

> "We're living in an era of rapid innovation, and honestly I don't know yet whether AI is good or bad. But I think it's similar to the **internet era** before: some jobs disappeared, but many new jobs were also created. AI will probably be the same — it will replace some jobs and introduce new ones. So instead of fearing it, we should learn to adapt and use it well."

> Why it works: it sounds **balanced and confident**, and the history comparison is hard to argue against. Great as your final sentence.
