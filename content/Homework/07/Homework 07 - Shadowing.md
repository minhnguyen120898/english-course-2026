## 📜 Full Text

> Get up and start the day. Do not **lie in bed**. Do not **hit the snooze button**. Lying in bed is making how you feel worse — and so, how do you get out of bed? First, you can use my Five-Second Rule; it is super simple. As soon as the alarm rings, you're just gonna **count backwards**: 5, 4, 3, 2, 1 — boom! Get out of bed. And if you hesitate for more than five seconds, you actually will **lose all motivation** to do it.

---

## 🔑 Key Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
| ------ | ------- |
| **Lie in bed** | Stay lying down in bed (not getting up) |
| **Hit the snooze button** | Press snooze to delay the alarm |
| **Count backwards** | Say numbers in reverse: 5, 4, 3, 2, 1 |
| **Hesitate** | Pause because you're unsure |
| **Lose all motivation** | Completely stop wanting to do something |
| **gonna** | Casual spoken form of "going to" |

## 🗣️ Shadowing Tips — Sound Natural

**Connected speech (link the words):**

- _get up_ → "ge-**dup**"
- _lie in bed_ → "lie-**yin**-bed"
- _hit the_ → "hi-**the**"
- _count backwards_ → "count-**backwards**" (one smooth push)
- _get out of bed_ → "ge-**dout**-of-bed"

**Stress the strong words** (the meaning words), say the small words quickly:

> Get **up** and **start** the **day**. Do **not** **lie** in **bed**.

**Rhythm & pauses** — pause where the punctuation is:

> …making how you feel **worse** — _(pause)_ and so, how do you get out of bed? _(pause)_ First…

**The countdown** — say it with energy and speed up slightly:

> "**5… 4… 3… 2… 1 — boom!**" Then drop your voice for "Get out of bed."

---

## 🔁 Practice Routine

1. **Listen** to the whole clip once without speaking.
2. **Listen + read** along silently, watching the linked sounds above.
3. **Shadow** — speak at the same time as the speaker, copying their rhythm (3–4 times).
4. **Record yourself** saying it alone, then compare with the original.
5. Re-record until it feels smooth and natural, then send to Zalo.

> 💡 Don't aim for a perfect accent — aim for the same **rhythm, stress, and energy** as the speaker. That's what makes shadowing work.
