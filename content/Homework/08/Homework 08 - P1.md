## Q1. Is it okay to live with your partner before marriage?

In my opinion, yes, it's okay to live with your partner before marriage. Living together helps two people understand each other more deeply — how they share housework, money, and daily habits. It's better to discover a serious misunderstanding before the wedding than after it. Of course, both families should agree first, because in Vietnam this is still quite sensitive for some parents.

---

## Q2. What do you think is the best age to get married? Why?

I think the best age to get married is around twenty-eight to thirty-ish. By that age, most people have a stable job and enough life experience to handle the responsibilities of a family. They are mature enough to solve problems calmly instead of arguing over small things. That said, there's no perfect age — what really matters is being ready and finding the right person to grow old together with.

---

## Q3. After you get married, would you live with your parents or your in-laws? Why?

Honestly, I would prefer to live separately if we can afford it, so my partner and I have our own private space. Living with parents or in-laws can be convenient — they can help with the children — but it can also cause misunderstandings between two generations. If living apart isn't possible at first, I would choose whichever home is more comfortable for both of us, and visit our families often.
