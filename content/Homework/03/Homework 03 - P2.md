This is the VOA Special English Economics Reports. [India]{/ˈɪn.di.ə/} is traditionally a tea-drinking country. But Indians are gaining a new [taste]{ /teɪst/} for coffee. This has led international coffee companies to consider moving into the market. At the same time, local business people are looking for new ways to profit from the country's tea-drinking tradition. They are opening new places that offer tea. Coffee shops have spread from major cities like New Delhi and Mumbai to smaller towns. In the past 10 years, cafes have become [increasingly]{ /ɪnˈkriː.sɪŋ.li/} [popular]{/ˈpɑː.pjə.lɚ/}. Indian's huge population of young people has  quickly taken to the coffee culture.
