## Q1. What is the most famous specialty of your hometown? How do you make it?

The most famous specialty of my hometown is Mỳ Phú Chiêm, a special variant of Mỳ Quảng from Quảng Nam province. Mỳ Phú Chiêm is considered the [cradle]{ /ˈkreɪ.dəl/} of Mỳ Quảng. The difference is that Mỳ Phú Chiêm stands out with a rich [broth]{/brɑːθ/} cooked from ground [shrimp]{ /ʃrɪmp/} and [crab]{/kræb/} [paste]{ /peɪst/}, while regular Mỳ Quảng uses bone broth. It is typically served with fresh raw vegetables, fresh chilli or chilli sauce, and grilled rice paper.

## Q2. Why do you think some people choose to be vegetarians?

I think some people become [vegetarians]{/ˌvedʒ.əˈter.i.ən/} because they want to cut down on fatty meat and reduce their [cholesterol]{/kəˈles.tə.rɑːl/}. Another reason might be that they feel bad about killing animals, or it could be due to [religion]{ /rɪˈlɪdʒ.ən/} — some religions do not allow eating meat. Personally, I could never be a vegetarian because I love food and I want to try every food I [possibly]{/ˈpɑː.sə.bli/} can!

## Q3. Describe what you think is a healthy diet.

I think a healthy diet should include a balance of [portions]{ /ˈpɔːr.ʃən/}, water, and [nutrients]{ /ˈnuː.tri.ənt/}. Balance is the key — we should eat a balanced diet with the right amount of protein, [vitamins]{ /ˈvaɪ.t̬ə-/}, and [minerals]{/ˈmɪn.ər.əl/}. We should also control our portions and [maintain]{ /meɪnˈteɪn/} good hydration throughout the day. We can cut down on oily and high-fat food, but it is okay to have a [guilty pleasure]{/ˌɡɪl.ti ˈpleʒ.ɚ/} sometimes — after all, balance is key!
