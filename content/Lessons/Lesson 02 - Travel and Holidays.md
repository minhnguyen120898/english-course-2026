# 📚 Lesson 02 — Travel and Holidays

**Course:** English Lower Intermediate **Topic:** Travel & Holidays **Tags:** #english #vocabulary #travel #lower-intermediate

## 📖 Vocabulary

### 1. Tourist Trap

- **Type:** (n) — _idiom_
- **Meaning:** A place that attracts many tourists but is overpriced or low quality.
- **Example:** _"That restaurant near the Eiffel Tower is a total tourist trap — the food was terrible and so expensive!"_

---

### 2. Budget Airline

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A cheap airline that offers low prices but fewer services (no free food, smaller seats, extra fees for bags).
- **Example:** _"We flew with a budget airline to save money, but we had to pay extra for our luggage."_

---

### 3. Baggage Claim

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** The area in an airport where you collect your checked luggage after a flight.
- **Example:** _"After landing, we waited 30 minutes at baggage claim for our suitcases."_

---

### 4. Compensation

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Money or something else given to you because of a problem, delay, or loss.
- **Example:** _"The airline gave us $200 compensation because our flight was delayed by 5 hours."_

---

### 5. Customs & Immigration

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:**
    - **Customs** — the checkpoint where officers check what goods you are bringing into a country.
    - **Immigration** — the checkpoint where officers check your passport and visa.
- **Example:** _"We spent an hour going through customs and immigration before we could leave the airport."_

---

### 6. Travel Insurance

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Insurance you buy before a trip to cover problems like lost bags, cancelled flights, or medical emergencies.
- **Example:** _"Always buy travel insurance — she broke her leg on holiday and it saved her thousands of dollars."_

---

### 7. Layover / Stopover

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A stop in a city between two flights.
    - **Layover** — usually short (a few hours)
    - **Stopover** — longer, sometimes overnight
- **Example:** _"We had a 6-hour layover in Singapore, so we left the airport to explore the city."_

---

### 8. Peak Season

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** The most popular and busiest time of year for travel, usually more expensive.
- **Example:** _"Flights to Europe during peak season are twice the price compared to winter."_

---

### 9. Flight Tracker

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A website or app that shows real-time information about flights — where they are, delays, arrival times.
- **Example:** _"I used a flight tracker to check if my friend's plane had landed yet."_

---

### 10. Itinerary

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A detailed plan or schedule of your trip — where you go, when, and what you do.
- **Example:** _"My travel itinerary for Japan included Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka over 10 days."_

---

### 11. Vlog

- **Type:** (n / v)
- **Meaning:** A video blog — a video diary where someone records and shares their travel experiences online.
- **Example:** _"She vlogs all her trips and posts them on YouTube — she has over 500,000 subscribers."_

---

### 12. Flight Attendant

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A person who works on a plane to serve passengers and ensure their safety.
- **Example:** _"The flight attendant showed us how to use the safety equipment before takeoff."_

---

### 13. Global Citizen

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A person who feels connected to the whole world, not just their own country, and respects all cultures.
- **Example:** _"After living in five different countries, she truly sees herself as a global citizen."_

---

## 💬 Expressions & Phrases

### 1. I Own It

- **Meaning:** I take full responsibility for something — a mistake, a decision, or an achievement.
- **Example:** _"I got lost because I didn't check the map. I own it."_

---

### 2. Someone Tries to Rip You Off

- **Meaning:** Someone tries to cheat you by charging too much money.
- **Example:** _"The taxi driver tried to rip me off by taking the longest route to the hotel."_

---

### 3. Materials Decide Your Actions

- **Meaning:** The things you have (money, resources) control what you can do.
- **Example:** _"I want to travel more, but materials decide your actions — I need to save first."_

---

### 4. Broaden My Horizons

- **Meaning:** To expand your knowledge, experience, or way of thinking.
- **Example:** _"I traveled to India to broaden my horizons and learn about a completely different culture."_

---

### 5. Escape from My Daily Routine

- **Meaning:** To take a break from the normal, repetitive activities of everyday life.
- **Example:** _"I booked a holiday in Bali just to escape from my daily routine."_

---

### 6. Experience Different Cultures

- **Meaning:** To learn about and participate in the traditions, food, and lifestyle of other countries.
- **Example:** _"One of my main reasons for traveling is to experience different cultures firsthand."_

---

### 7. Explore New Destinations

- **Meaning:** To visit and discover places you have never been before.
- **Example:** _"Every year I try to explore new destinations instead of going back to the same place."_

---

### 8. Step Out of My Comfort Zone

- **Meaning:** To do something unfamiliar or challenging that makes you nervous.
- **Example:** _"Traveling alone for the first time really helped me step out of my comfort zone."_

---

### 9. Cost an Arm and a Leg

- **Meaning:** To be extremely expensive.
- **Example:** _"Flying business class costs an arm and a leg, so I always book economy."_
