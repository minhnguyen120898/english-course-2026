## 📖 Vocabulary

### 1. Have a Crush on

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To have a romantic feeling for someone (often secret).
- **Example:** _"I have a crush on a girl in my class, but I haven't talked to her yet."_

---

### 2. Blind Date

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A date between two people who have never met, usually arranged by others.
- **Example:** _"Last week, I went on a blind date arranged by my friend."_

---

### 3. Ask Someone Out

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To invite someone on a date.
- **Example:** _"He finally asked her out after talking online for a month."_

---

### 4. I'm Taken / I'm in a Relationship

- **Type:** (phrase)
- **Meaning:** A way to say you already have a partner.
- **Example:** _"Sorry, I'm taken. I already have a boyfriend."_

---

### 5. Cuddle / Spoon

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To hold someone closely and affectionately. _Spoon_ = cuddle lying down, both facing the same way.
- **Example:** _"They like to cuddle while watching movies at home."_

---

### 6. Grow Old Together

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To spend your whole life with your partner until old age.
- **Example:** _"They hope to grow old together and build a happy family."_

---

### 7. Long-distance Relationship

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A romantic relationship where the partners live far apart.
- **Example:** _"It's not easy to maintain a long-distance relationship, but they try their best."_

---

### 8. Misunderstanding

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A failure to understand each other correctly; a small conflict from confusion.
- **Example:** _"They had a misunderstanding, but they solved it quickly."_

---

### 9. Trust Issues

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Difficulty trusting a partner, often because of past experiences.
- **Example:** _"He has trust issues because of his past relationship."_

---

### 10. Toxic Relationship

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** An unhealthy relationship that harms your wellbeing or mental health.
- **Example:** _"She decided to leave a toxic relationship to protect her mental health."_

---

### 11. Move On

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To recover emotionally and continue your life after a breakup.
- **Example:** _"It took him a long time to move on after the breakup."_

---

### 12. Settle Down

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To start a stable life, usually with marriage and a home.
- **Example:** _"After many years of dating, they want to settle down."_

## 📐 Grammar Corner — Articles: a / an / the

| Article  | Use it for…                              |
| -------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **a / an** | First time mentioned / one of many / not specific |
| **the**  | Known / specific / already mentioned     |

> Use **an** before a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u): _an office, an apple._

**a / an** (first time, not specific):

- _I saw **a** dog near my house._
- _She works in **an** office._
- _I'm looking for **a** job._

**the** (known or specific — we already know which one):

- _**The** dog was very friendly._ (the same dog from before)
- _**The** office is near **the** river._
- _**The** job starts next month._

> 💡 Pattern: introduce something with **a/an**, then switch to **the** once it's known.
> _"I bought **a** cake. **The** cake was delicious."_

## 🗣️ Speaking Tip — "-ish"

> Add **-ish** to mean _approximately / not exact / kind of_ — after a time, number, or adjective.

- _"When will you finish work?" → "Five-ish."_ (around 5)
- _"Is he tall?" → "Tall-ish."_ (kind of tall)
- _"Are you free tonight?" → "Free-ish."_ (more or less free)

## 💬 Practice Questions

1. Should a guy text a woman he is interested in every day? Why or why not?
2. Is it okay to live with your partner before marriage?
3. Is it acceptable for a woman to take the initiative with a man she likes?
4. Do you remember your first date? Where did you go?
5. Do you think online dating is a good way to find true love? Why or why not?
6. Do your parents need to approve of the person you marry? Why or why not?
7. After you get married, would you live with your parents or your in-laws? Why?
8. What is one secret to a happy and successful marriage?
9. What is one main reason couples argue?
10. Can exes stay friends? Why or why not?
11. Why do you think some people prefer to stay single?
