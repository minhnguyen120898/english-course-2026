## 📖 Vocabulary

### 1. Touch Base

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To briefly connect or check in with someone.
- **Example:** _"Let's touch base later today and see how the project is going."_

---

### 2. Give Someone a Heads-up

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To warn or tell someone about something in advance.
- **Example:** _"I'll give you a heads-up if I see the boss coming."_

---

### 3. On the Same Page

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To share the same understanding or agreement.
- **Example:** _"Let's make sure we're on the same page before we start."_

---

### 4. Back-to-back Meetings

- **Type:** (adj phr)
- **Meaning:** Meetings scheduled one right after another with no break.
- **Example:** _"I have back-to-back meetings all morning, so I'm free after lunch."_

---

### 5. Take Initiative

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To act or solve a problem without being told to.
- **Example:** _"She took initiative and fixed the problem without being asked."_

---

### 6. Gig Job / Gig Economy

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Short-term or freelance work instead of a permanent job; the economy built on it.
- **Example:** _"He prefers to work as a freelance content creator as his gig job."_

---

### 7. Sort Something Out

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To solve or deal with a problem.
- **Example:** _"Don't worry, Thao will sort it out this afternoon."_

---

### 8. Flag an Issue

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To point out or raise a problem so others notice it.
- **Example:** _"I want to flag an issue with the delivery schedule."_

---

### 9. Drop the Ball

- **Type:** (idiom)
- **Meaning:** To make a mistake by forgetting or failing to do something.
- **Example:** _"Minh dropped the ball and forgot to send the email."_

---

### 10. Catch Up with Someone

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To meet and talk, often to share updates.
- **Example:** _"Let's catch up tomorrow and talk about the report."_

---

### 11. Tight Deadline

- **Type:** (adj + n)
- **Meaning:** Very little time to finish a task.
- **Example:** _"We're working to a tight deadline this week."_

---

### 12. Meeting Minutes / Meeting Agenda

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:**
    - **Agenda** — the list of topics planned *before* a meeting.
    - **Minutes** — the written notes of what was said *after* a meeting.
- **Example:** _"After the meeting, I wrote the meeting minutes and sent them to everyone."_

---

### 13. Swamped

- **Type:** (adj)
- **Meaning:** Extremely busy; overloaded with work.
- **Example:** _"I'm swamped today, so I'll reply later."_

---

### 14. Juggle Tasks

- **Type:** (phr)
- **Meaning:** To handle many tasks at the same time.
- **Example:** _"She's juggling tasks and answering emails at the same time."_

---

## 📐 Grammar Corner — "Used to"

> Use **used to** to talk about a **past habit or situation that is not true now**.

**Positive:** _used to_ + base verb

- _I **used to** live with my parents, but now I live alone._
- _She **used to** work nights, but now she works days._
- _We **used to** go out every weekend._

**Negative:** _didn't_ + **use to** + base verb (no "d"!)

- _I didn't **use to** like coffee._
- _He didn't **use to** exercise much._

> ⚠️ Common mistake: in the negative/question, it's **use to** (no "d"), because "did" already shows the past.

---

## 🗣️ Speaking Tip — "No way!"

> Use it to show **strong surprise** or **strong disagreement** in a natural, spoken way.

- _"No way! You worked all weekend?"_ (surprise)
- _"No way, that's way too expensive."_ (disagreement)
- _"No way I'm getting up at 5 a.m."_ (refusal)

## 💬 Practice Questions

1. What part of your job causes you the most stress? Why?
2. Do you need to work with other colleagues to complete your tasks?
3. Do you always finish your tasks on time and with good quality?
4. What motivates you to complete your tasks at work?
5. Have you ever wanted to quit a job? What stopped you?
6. Do you have to work with someone you don't like, or someone who doesn't like you?
7. If you could choose your boss, would you choose your current one? Why or why not?
8. How do you react when your boss reprimands you?
9. Do you proactively share your ideas in meetings with your boss?
10. What's one mistake you've made at work that you learned from?
11. Does your current job help you achieve your life goals?
12. How important are personal relationships at work?

2. When looking for a new job, which factors are most important to you? **Rank them:** salary, benefits, work environment, boss, location, company reputation.
