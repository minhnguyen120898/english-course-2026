## 📖 Vocabulary

### 1. Traffic Congestion

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A situation where roads are so full of vehicles that traffic moves very slowly or stops completely.
- **Vietnamese:** Tắc đường / ùn tắc giao thông
- **Example:** _"There was heavy traffic congestion in Hai Chau district this morning."_

---

### 2. Intersection

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A point where two or more roads cross each other. Also called a "crossroads" (British English) or "junction".
- **Vietnamese:** Ngã tư / giao lộ
- **Example:** _"Turn left at the intersection."_

---

### 3. Pedestrian

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A person who is walking on a road or in a town — not in a vehicle.
- **Vietnamese:** Người đi bộ
- **Example:** _"The pedestrian crossed the street safely."_

---

### 4. Rush Hour

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** The time of day — usually morning and evening — when many people travel to or from work, causing heavy traffic.
- **Vietnamese:** Giờ cao điểm
- **Example:** _"The streets are very crowded during rush hour."_

---

### 5. Commute

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To travel regularly between home and work or school, usually every day.
- **Vietnamese:** Đi lại hàng ngày / di chuyển thường xuyên
- **Example:** _"I commute to work by motorbike."_
- **Note:** _"Commute" is specifically for regular home ↔ work travel. "Travel" is more general._

---

### 6. Public Transport

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Buses, trains, and other forms of transport available to everyone, usually run by the government.
- **Vietnamese:** Phương tiện giao thông công cộng
- **Example:** _"She uses public transport every day."_
- **Note:** _British English: "public transport" | American English: "public transportation"_

---

### 7. Overcrowded

- **Type:** (adj)
- **Meaning:** Containing too many people — more than the space comfortably allows.
- **Vietnamese:** Quá đông / quá tải
- **Example:** _"The bus was so overcrowded that I had to stand the whole way home."_

---

### 8. Road Rage

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Aggressive or violent behaviour by a driver who is angry at another road user.
- **Vietnamese:** Nổi giận khi lái xe / hành vi hung hãn trên đường
- **Example:** _"He got road rage when another driver cut him off at the roundabout."_
- **Note:** _"Cut someone off" = suddenly move in front of another vehicle without warning._

---

### 9. Dash Cam

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A small camera mounted on a car's dashboard that continuously records video while driving.
- **Vietnamese:** Camera hành trình
- **Example:** _"Our dash cam recorded the accident and we sent the video to the police."_

---

### 10. Beat the Traffic

- **Type:** (phrase)
- **Meaning:** To leave early enough to travel before traffic becomes heavy, avoiding delays.
- **Vietnamese:** Tránh kẹt xe
- **Example:** _"We left early to beat the traffic."_

---

### 11. Run Late

- **Type:** (phrase)
- **Meaning:** To be behind schedule; arriving or doing something later than planned.
- **Vietnamese:** Bị trễ / đến muộn
- **Example:** _"Sorry, I'm stuck in traffic and running late today."_

---

### 12. Take a Shortcut

- **Type:** (phrase)
- **Meaning:** To use a quicker or shorter route than the normal one.
- **Vietnamese:** Đi đường tắt
- **Example:** _"Let's take a shortcut through the alley."_

---

### 13. Break Down

- **Type:** (phrasal verb)
- **Meaning:** When a vehicle or machine stops working because of a mechanical problem.
- **Vietnamese:** Hỏng xe / xe bị hỏng giữa đường
- **Example:** _"Our bus broke down on the highway, so we arrived almost an hour late."_

---

### 14. Accelerate / Decelerate

- **Type:** (v) — opposites
- **Meaning:**
    - **Accelerate** — to increase speed. **Vietnamese:** tăng tốc
    - **Decelerate** — to reduce speed. **Vietnamese:** giảm tốc
- **Example:** _"The car accelerated quickly when the light turned green."_
- **Example:** _"The train began to decelerate as it approached the station."_

---

### 15. Run a Red Light

- **Type:** (phrase)
- **Meaning:** To drive through a red traffic light without stopping — illegal and dangerous.
- **Vietnamese:** Vượt đèn đỏ
- **Example:** _"We often see motorbikes run a red light — but it is very dangerous."_

---

### 16. Pull Over

- **Type:** (v / phrasal verb)
- **Meaning:** To move a vehicle to the side of the road and stop, usually when asked by police.
- **Vietnamese:** Dừng xe vào lề đường
- **Example:** _"The police asked me to pull over to check my licence."_

## 🔊 Pronunciation — "TH" Sounds (/θ/ and /ð/)

Both sounds use the same tongue position: **tongue lightly between your teeth**.

| Sound    | Symbol | Voice? | Examples                                      |
| -------- | ------ | ------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| Unvoiced | /θ/    | ❌ No  | think, thank, three, thirty, Thursday, theory |
| Voiced   | /ð/    | ✅ Yes | this, that, these, those, they, them          |

**Tip:** Place your finger on your throat. For /ð/ you feel a vibration. For /θ/ you feel nothing — just air.

## 🗣️ Speaking Tip

> **On the one hand … (but) on the other hand …**

Use this to present **two sides of an idea** — perfect for discussion and debate questions.

- _"On the one hand, motorbikes are cheap and flexible. On the other hand, they are dangerous and polluting."_
- _"On the one hand, public transport reduces congestion. On the other hand, it is not always convenient."_

### Role Play — Traffic Violation 🚔

**Situation:** A police officer pulls you over for a traffic violation.

**Student A — Police Officer:**
- You believe the driver broke a traffic law
- Ask questions and explain the situation
- Decide: give a warning or a fine?

**Student B — Driver:**
- You were stopped by the police
- Explain your situation and give a reason
- Try to avoid or reduce the fine

**Useful phrases:**
- _"Excuse me, do you know why I pulled you over?"_
- _"May I see your licence and registration, please?"_
- _"I'm sorry, officer. I didn't realise I..."_
- _"I was running late and I..."_
- _"I'm going to give you a warning this time, but..."_
