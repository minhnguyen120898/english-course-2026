## 📖 Vocabulary

### 1. Spouse

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Your husband or wife; the person you are legally married to.
- **Example:** _"Her spouse travels a lot for work, so she manages the house alone."_

---

### 2. Sibling

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A brother or sister.
- **Example:** _"I have two siblings — one older brother and one younger sister."_

---

### 3. Twins

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Two children born at the same time from the same mother.
- **Example:** _"The twins look exactly alike — I can never tell them apart!"_

---

### 4. Ancestor Worship

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A tradition of showing deep respect and honor to family members who have died, common in many Asian cultures.
- **Example:** _"In Vietnam, ancestor worship is an important part of the culture, especially during Tết."_

---

### 5. In-laws

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** The family of your husband or wife (e.g., mother-in-law, father-in-law).
- **Example:** _"We visit my in-laws every Sunday for dinner."_

---

### 6. Black Sheep

- **Type:** (n) — _idiom_
- **Meaning:** A person in a family who is different from the others, often in a negative or embarrassing way.
- **Example:** _"He dropped out of school and never got a job — he's considered the black sheep of the family."_

---

### 7. Breadwinner

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** The person in a family who earns most or all of the money.
- **Example:** _"After her husband lost his job, she became the main breadwinner."_

---

### 8. Adopt

- **Type:** (v)
- **Meaning:** To legally take someone else's child and raise them as your own.
- **Example:** _"They decided to adopt a baby girl from an orphanage."_

---

### 9. Role Model

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A person whose behavior and values others look up to and want to copy.
- **Example:** _"My father is my role model — he worked hard and always treated people with kindness."_

---

### 10. Collectivism / Individualism

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:**
    - **Collectivism** — a belief that the group (family, community) is more important than the individual.
    - **Individualism** — a belief that personal freedom and independence are most important.
- **Example:** _"Asian cultures often value collectivism, while Western cultures tend to emphasize individualism."_

---

### 11. Fall Out

- **Type:** (phrasal verb)
- **Meaning:** To have a serious argument with someone and stop being friends or close.
- **Example:** _"She fell out with her sister over money and they didn't speak for a year."_

---

### 12. Immediate Family

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Your closest family members — parents, siblings, spouse, and children.
- **Example:** _"Only immediate family members were invited to the private ceremony."_

---

### 13. Extended Family

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A larger family group that includes grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins, etc.
- **Example:** _"During Tết, our extended family gathers together at my grandparents' house."_

---

### 14. Family Reunion

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A special gathering where family members — often from different places — come together to celebrate and catch up.
- **Example:** _"We have a big family reunion every summer with over 50 relatives."_
