## 📖 Vocabulary

### 1. High School Diploma

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** An official certificate you receive when you successfully finish high school.
- **Example:** _"After obtaining my high school diploma, I decided to pursue higher education instead of entering the workforce immediately."_

---

### 2. Gap Year

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A year taken off from studying — usually between high school and university — to travel, work, or gain real-world experience.
- **Example:** _"I took a gap year to gain real-world experience and figure out what I truly wanted to study."_

---

### 3. Community College

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A lower-cost, two-year college that offers basic degrees and courses, often before transferring to a university.
- **Example:** _"Starting at a community college was a practical choice, as it allowed me to save money before transferring to a university."_

---

### 4. Trade / Vocational School

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A school that teaches practical, hands-on skills for specific jobs — like electricians, mechanics, or chefs — instead of academic subjects.
- **Example:** _"Vocational schools are ideal for students who prefer hands-on training over academic study."_

---

### 5. Transfer to the University

- **Type:** (v. phr)
- **Meaning:** To move from one school or college to a university to continue your studies.
- **Example:** _"After two years at community college, she transferred to the university."_

---

### 6. Associate's Degree

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A two-year degree awarded by a community college — the step before a bachelor's degree.
- **Example:** _"After completing my associate's degree, I transferred to the university to continue my studies in business administration."_

---

### 7. Bachelor's Degree (BA / BS)

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A four-year university degree — the most common undergraduate qualification.
    - **BA** = Bachelor of Arts (humanities, languages, social sciences)
    - **BS** = Bachelor of Science (science, technology, engineering)
- **Example:** _"I'm currently working toward a bachelor's degree, which I believe will open up more career opportunities."_

---

### 8. Master's Degree (MA / MS)

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** A postgraduate degree completed after a bachelor's, usually taking 1–2 years. Shows deeper knowledge in a specific field.
- **Example:** _"In the future, I may pursue a master's degree to deepen my knowledge in my field."_

---

### 9. PhD / Doctorate Degree

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** The highest level of academic degree — usually requires several years of original research.
- **Example:** _"Although a PhD requires a long-term commitment, it can be rewarding for those interested in research."_

---

### 10. Scholarship

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** Money given to a student to help pay for their education, usually based on academic achievement or financial need — does not need to be paid back.
- **Example:** _"Receiving a scholarship significantly reduced my financial burden and motivated me to study harder."_

---

### 11. Financial Aid / Loan

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:**
    - **Financial aid** — money given by the government or school to help students pay for education.
    - **Loan** — money borrowed that must be paid back later, often with interest.
- **Example:** _"Many students rely on financial aid or take out loans to afford tuition fees."_

---

### 12. Semester

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:** One of the two main periods that divide an academic year, usually about 4–5 months each.
- **Example:** _"This semester has been quite demanding, as I have to balance multiple assignments and exams."_

---

### 13. Theoretical vs Practical Learning

- **Type:** (n)
- **Meaning:**
    - **Theoretical learning** — learning concepts, ideas, and knowledge from books and lectures.
    - **Practical learning** — learning by doing, applying knowledge in real situations.
- **Example:** _"While theoretical learning provides a solid foundation, practical experience is essential in today's job market."_

---

### 14. Keep Up With

- **Type:** (v. phr)
- **Meaning:** To maintain the same pace or level as something — to not fall behind.
- **Example:** _"It can be challenging to keep up with the fast pace of university courses."_

---

### 15. Fall Behind

- **Type:** (v. phr)
- **Meaning:** To make less progress than expected — to be slower than others or behind schedule.
- **Example:** _"If students don't manage their time effectively, they may quickly fall behind."_

---

## 🗣️ Speaking Tip

> **"In all likelihood..."**

Use this phrase to say something is **very likely** to happen. Sounds confident and natural!

- _"In all likelihood, he will be late."_
- _"In all likelihood, online learning will become more common in the future."_
- _"In all likelihood, a university degree is still important in Vietnam's job market."_

## 💬 Discussion Questions

1. When you chose your major, did you research the job market for it?
2. Why is learning a new language important?
3. Is it better to attend a private school or a public school in Vietnam?
4. Are international schools popular in Vietnam?
5. Should foreign languages be required in schools?
6. What are the differences between the Vietnamese and Western education systems?
7. What qualities make a good teacher?
8. Online learning vs. in-class learning — what are the pros and cons?
9. If you do not get a university degree, do you lose face with your family and friends?
10. **Debate:** Vocational training vs. a university degree — which is more useful?
